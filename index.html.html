Insights & Projection

What this growth means
What improved (and why it matters):
December proves the campaign can scale with control. Visibility expanded dramatically (reach and impressions), clicks increased 4.8x, and lead volume grew while CPL dropped. This combination indicates stronger distribution without losing conversion efficiency — the healthiest signal when increasing budget.
  • Leads up (+37%) while CPL down (−6.5%) → better efficiency.
  • Click volume up (+386%) → traffic capacity increased.
  • Scale effects naturally reduce CTR; the key is cost-to-lead (which improved).
What to watch (and what to do next):
CTR dropped and profile visits slightly decreased — this suggests the campaign reached broader audiences. The next performance jump will come from improving “click → lead” conversion and tightening creative messaging.
  • Launch 3 creative angles: athletes recovery, seniors pain relief, back pain support.
  • Optimize landing flow: shorter form, stronger headline, clearer CTA.
  • Run remarketing for clickers to increase leads without proportional spend.
Projection: if we keep December’s scale and improve click-to-lead conversion, lead volume should rise without CPL increasing — turning reach growth into predictable demand.
